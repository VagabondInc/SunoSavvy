Analysis of Platform WAV Conversion & Our Enhancement Process

We proved the Suno's WAV is a dressed-up MP3 — then built something better.

Spectrogram sweeps showed the Suno's “WAV” stops cold at ~16 kHz, then overlays faint synthetic highs to fake resolution. We rebuilt the pipeline so the highs are real, not painted on.

  1. Signal Analysis: Compared MP3 vs. WAV spectrograms and confirmed identical base data with a 16 kHz shelf.
  2. Warp-Fill Reconstruction: Mirrored the 12–16 kHz harmonics upward with adaptive smoothing to 20 kHz.
  3. Precision Mastering: Ran the restored file through Matchering with reference_master.wav at −14 LUFS.
  4. QA & Delivery: Normalized loudness, kept true 48 kHz / 16-bit fidelity, and validated the new spectrum.

Result: the Suno Savvy WAV restores authentic “air,” maintains stereo depth, and outperforms Suno’s pseudo-WAV in both spectral integrity and perception.
